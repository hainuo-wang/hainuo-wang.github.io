About

About Me

I am a direct Ph.D. student at Tianjin University, advised by Prof. Xiaojie Guo. My research focuses on computer vision, especially image generation, diffusion models, and low-level vision. I welcome discussions and collaborations on related research topics.

News

Recent Updates

  1. 2026.05 Two preprints were released: Representative Attention for Vision Transformers, which studies representation-space token compression for linear attention, and Internally Referenced Low-Light Enhancement, a self-supervised framework for low-light image enhancement.
  2. 2026.04 Released On the Global Photometric Alignment for Low-Level Vision, a study of photometric inconsistency in paired low-level vision supervision and a closed-form alignment loss for restoration training.
  3. 2026.03 Released WiT: Waypoint Diffusion Transformers, a pixel-space diffusion framework that uses semantic waypoints to reduce trajectory conflict, together with the official project page.
  4. 2026.02 Rectifying Latent Space for Generative Single-Image Reflection Removal accepted to CVPR 2026.
  5. 2025.09 MODEM accepted to NeurIPS 2025. The method introduces Morton-order degradation estimation for adverse-weather image recovery.
  6. 2025.06 Won two champion titles as a team member in the NTIRE 2025 Single Image Reflection Removal in the Wild Challenge and the NTIRE 2025 Image Shadow Removal Challenge at CVPR 2025.
  7. 2025.03 Developed XReflection, an open-source toolbox for single-image reflection removal, including training, inference, and challenge-oriented restoration workflows.
  8. 2024.09 Single Image Reflection Separation via Dual-Stream Interactive Transformers accepted to NeurIPS 2024.
  9. 2024.09 Started direct Ph.D. study at Tianjin University.
